The Audit Method

A quiet look at your public online footprint.

No login, no penetration testing, no anything invasive. Just a careful read of what the internet already says about your business — and what to do about it.

Ethics-first, by design

We never attempt unauthorised access, password testing, or any invasive cybersecurity activity. Audits are passive: only data that's already public is examined. For deeper security work, we require formal written authorisation. This is non-negotiable.

The process

Four careful steps. Forty signals.

Whether you're getting the free snapshot or the full report, we follow the same disciplined method. Here's what happens.

1

Gather public signals

We collect what's publicly visible: website, Google profile, social bios, directory listings, online reviews. No tools touch your servers.

2

Score against benchmarks

40+ signals across 5 categories: SEO, performance, trust, lead capture, and brand consistency. Scored against industry benchmarks and your local competitors.

3

Write a readable report

Plain English. Visuals where they help. Issues ranked by impact. Even your non-technical co-founder will be able to read it cover-to-cover.

4

Recommend & prioritise

For each issue: what to fix, why it matters, an effort estimate, and whether you should DIY, hire local, or call us. No vague "you need a strategy" fluff.

A note on scope

The free snapshot is designed for Nigerian SMEs and mid-market businesses across hospitality, real estate, professional services, retail, and education. For regulated sectors — banking, government, healthcare, capital markets, regulated telecom — engagements begin with a signed scope-of-work and operate within the institution's specific compliance framework (CBN, NITDA, NCC, SEC, NDPR as applicable). We don't run unsolicited audits on regulated entities. If you represent one, get in touch directly and we'll structure the engagement properly.
